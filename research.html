Research Projects

I have participated in several interdisciplinary projects across a range of fields—including political science, economics, law, and public policy—in collaboration with scholars from universities in the UK, the US, the Netherlands, Germany, Spain, Greece and Turkey.

Breaking news, breaking barriers?

Principal Investigator. A Fair and Resilient Societies UvA funded project (€40,000) exploring media representations of immigrants through journalistic and citizen lenses with a specific focus on the perceptions of skills, in collaboration with Kathleen Beckers (Media Studies) and Maja Hertoghs (Anthropology).

Understanding the Just Transition

Co-Investigator. An ENLENS UvA funded project (€45,000) examining energy transition through wellbeing, justice, and fairness perspectives, with Matteo Fermeglia (Law), Colin Hickey (Political Philosophy) and Christina Eckes (Law).

COVIDEU

Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Humboldt University of Berlin (2022-2023), studying how crises affect the EU, with a focus on how to design policy instruments to address a crisis, how to coordinate policy responses among member states and how to communicate to the public are questions that are of utmost relevance to any future crisis. In collaboration with Heike Klüver (Political Science), Sara Hobolt (Political Science), Theresa Kuhn (Political Science), Toni Rodon (Political Science), Michal Krawczyk (Economics), Lisa Herbig (Political Science) and Irene Rodríguez (Political Science).

Register to Vote

Project Member. Series of digital field experiments on voter registration through social media and text messages in the UK, in collaboration with Florian Foos (Political Science), Peter John (Public Policy), and Vanessa Cheng-Matsuno (Public Policy).

Multidimensional Policy Responses to COVID-19

Project Member. Collaborative project funded by King's Together Award (£18,424) examining health-wealth preferences and policy implications during the pandemic, together with Shaun Hargreaves Heap (Economics), Christel Koop (Public Policy), Kostas Matakos (Political Economy) and Nina Weber (Political Economy).
